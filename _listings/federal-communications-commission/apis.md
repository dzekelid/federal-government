name: Federal Communications Commission
description: The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) is an independent agency
  of the United States government, created by Congressional statute (see 47 U.S.C.
  sect; 151 and 47 U.S.C. sect; 154) to regulate interstate and international communications
  by radio, television, wire, satellite, and cable in all 50 states, the District
  of Columbia and U.S. territories. The FCC works towards six goals in the areas of
  broadband, competition, the spectrum, the media, public safety and homeland security.
  The Commission is also in the process of modernizing itself.
